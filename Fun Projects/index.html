Analytical Field Ion Microscopy

Project Description

The phenomenon of extremely strong electric fields, reaching approximately 10 volts per nanometer, causing the rupture of atomic bonds has been extensively investigated in molecular chemistry. When this concept is applied to crystalline systems, particularly material surfaces, it is termed as field evaporation. Understanding the precise mechanisms of field evaporation becomes more complex when dealing with crystalline structures. Field evaporation serves as the fundamental process enabling techniques like atom probe tomography (APT) and 3D field ion microscopy (3D-FIM). Achieving atomically accurate reconstructions through APT relies heavily on a thorough comprehension of how ions form and detach from the surface at an atomic level. To gain insight into this phenomenon, we conduct first-principles calculations on faceted surfaces subjected to intense electric fields, focusing on both pure metals and alloys.
